To wonder about the nature of love is to consider love in its origin, in its forms and in its activities. What causes love? How can there be different types of love? What makes love last? What kind of actions does love inspire? Can we be friends with God? Does friendship require equality? Does sex interfere with friendship? Is friendship important for justice and for political entities? We pursue these questions primarily within the boundaries of philosophy.

COURSE REQUIREMENTS AND GRADING

            

10% Responsive Attendance. More than five physical absences subtracts an entire letter grade.


15% Class Preparation. At the beginning of each class, students are required to hand in a report on the text read for homework. These reports are not returned (so keep a copy for our class discussions and to help prepare for the quizzes). Late reports are not accepted. More than five missing reports subtracts an entire letter grade. These reports must be a product of your own work and not the work of another. These reports are not explicitly graded; however, excellent reports enable students to excel in class discussions, quizzes, and the exams. Each report should attempt to answer the following questions: 1) What point(s), if any, were especially surprising, interesting, or disagreeable? Explain what made the point an eye-opener. (2) What are the main points of the homework reading? 3) Should we agree or disagree with these main points? Explain. (4) Is the text a “keeper”? Explain. Your answers to these questions are especially important, not only because home work is the exercise that develops one’s mind, but because they are my way of knowing how difficult–and how interesting--you found the homework reading.


25% Wednesday quizzes — every week.


50% Two Exams.


Extra Credit. Periodically, there will be talks given for which one can earn extra credit by reporting on the speech. The report should summarize the speech and then answer the questions required for the homework reports. Extra-Credit work, if well-done, warms my heart and makes it likely that I will curve borderline grades up. You may feel free to recommend that a talk count for extra credit.
