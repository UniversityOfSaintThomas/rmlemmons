B) Optional: Phaedo pp. 500-511. These are difficult pages in which Socrates attempts to defeat Cebes’ argument by talking about the importance of causes and about the need for a substratum to underlie changes between opposites. For example, a cold pan of water turns into its opposite of hot water only because water is a substratum capable of being hot or cold and because water does not cause its own temperature. If water was the cause of its own heat, then the outside temperature would not make it cold. This would mean that water would not become hot then cold, cold then hot. It would always be hot. Likewise, since the soul is the cause of its own life, the soul cannot be a substratum capable of becoming dead and then alive, alive and then dead. This argument from causality refutes the earlier argument from opposites.
