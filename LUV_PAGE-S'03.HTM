"And the passion of love is not aroused suddenly, but is born of an earnest consideration of the object loved." 
Aquinas, Summa Theologiae II-II.27.2c

To wonder about the nature of love is to consider love in its origin, in its forms and in its activities. What causes love? How can there be different types of love? What makes love last? What kind of actions does love inspire? Can we be friends with God? Does friendship require equality? Does sex have an intrinsic meaning? We pursue these questions primarily--but not exclusively--within the boundaries of philosophy. This course will be structured as a seminar with students taking turns presenting analyses of the homework readings and posing discussion questions.
