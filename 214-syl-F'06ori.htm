Philosophical ethics not only explains and justifies morality, but seeks to establish the best basis for morality. The method of philosophical ethics is argument in search of insight; its hope is that evil will not triumph over the good. The overarching purpose of this course is to help us gain insight into ethical realities and consistency in moral reasoning. In addition, this course, while seeking to understand morality within and across cultures – emphasizes the insights of Aquinas and his natural law.

COURSE REQUIREMENTS AND GRADING

 


20% Class Participation and Preparation. Class preparation includes reflecting upon the following questions---unless, of course, other questions are specified: (1) What claim(s), if any, were especially surprising, interesting, or disagreeable? What made the claim(s) an eye-opener? (2) What are the main points of the homework reading? 3) Why should we agree or disagree with these main points? 4) What are the implications of the reading, if its claims are true? What are the implications if the claims are false? Your answers to these questions are especially important, not only because home work plays a key role in developing one’s mind, but because they prepare you to participate in class discussions. Particularly insightful questions or comments are especially appreciated. More than five absences drops your grade a letter.


40% Quizzes. Every two weeks on Wednesdays, beginning Sept. 20th. (10/4; 10/18;11/1;11/15;11/29;12/13)


40% Two Reflection Papers Plus One Student Presentation on One of the Papers---Your Choice of Topics and Presentation Dates---see Sequence of Topics and Readings. Each reflection paper is to be 3-4 typed pages each. Topics to be assigned. Each paper must not only argue that its position is true but it must also counter an objection. Each paper requires the argument to be outlined in an appendix. Each paper must integrate course materials with an insightful outside source.  Presentations should include one overhead slide.


Extra Credit. Periodically, there will be talks given for which one can earn extra credit by reporting on the speech. The report should summarize the speech and then answer the questions required for the homework reports. Extra-Credit work, if well-done, warms my heart and makes it likely that I will curve borderline grades up. You may feel free to recommend that a talk count for extra credit.
