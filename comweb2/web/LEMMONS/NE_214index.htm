ARISTOTLE'S NICOMACHEAN ETHICS: SELECTIONS

Homework assignments: Each team member is to read all the excerpts from the Nicomachean Ethics and post on the Internet a response to the questions assigned to their team. (It is possible that the answers to the following questions are scattered throughout the homework readings.) Two comments should also be posted by Tuesday, 9 pm.

Red Team (due Sat. 9 pm): (1) What is virtue? (2) What is "the mean'? (3) Why does Aristotle identify courage, temperance, liberality, magnificence, proper pride, good-temperedness, truthfulness, ready-wittedness, friendliness, modesty and righteous indignation as hitting the mean?  (4) Can any act hit the mean,  or are some acts always immoral?

Green Team (due Sunday 9 pm):  Morality concerns voluntary acts. (1) Is every type of ignorance involuntary? (2) Are all types of ignorance morally excusable, or does Aristotle argue that it is possible to become wicked and unjust by acting from  ignorance? (3) Why does Aristotle identify acts done in anger or appetite (desire) as voluntary?

Blue Team (due Monday 9 pm): (1) According to Aristotle why is the contemplation of God a better form of happiness than acting morally? (2) What is the gist of  Aristotle's Function Argument? (3) Does morality require contemplating God? (4) Does Aristotle agree with Plato that morality is pyschological health?
