CULTURAL ARTIFACT PRESENTATION

 

Themes central to this course are found in various popular songs, movies and advertising. Present to the class one of these themes, e.g. the cultural endorsement of life or the attempt to transform culture in favor of euthanasia. VCRs and cassette players will be available. If a song or an excerpt from a movie or a television show is shown, the lyrics or the video's dialogue must be printed out and distributed to the class.
 
 

IN-CLASS PRESENTATION PROCEDURE

1. Pass out copies of any dialogue whether in a song or in a video excerpt.

2. Play the song or the video excerpt; show the advertisement.

3. Explain the theme that you identified the artifact as expressing.

4. Present an argument about whether you consider American culture to be dominated by the theme captured by the artifact.

5. Hand in a summary of your presentation (one typed page). The summary should (1) identify the artifact and its theme and (2) present your argument about the prevalence of that theme within American culture.

Artifact grading will be based upon presentation interest, clarity, argumentation and depth of insight into American culture.
