INTRODUCTION: To wonder about the nature of love is to consider love in its origin, in its forms and in its activities. What causes love? How can there be different types of love? What makes love last? What kind of actions does love inspire? Can we be friends with God? Does friendship require equality? We pursue these questions primarily within the boundaries of philosophy. This course will be structured as a seminar with students taking turns presenting analyses of the homework readings and posing discussion questions.

TEXTS: Other Selves: Philosophers on Friendship edited by Michael Pakaluk; Friendship, A Study in Theological Ethics by Gilbert Meilaender; Love and Responsibility by Karol Wojtyla; ON-LINE TEXTS: Phaedrus; Symposium
