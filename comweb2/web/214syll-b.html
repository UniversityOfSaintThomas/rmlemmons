INTRODUCTION: The most pressing problem in the late 20th century is whether ethics is universal and relevant to the lives of individuals. This course focuses upon the question: Why Be Moral? It examines the fundamental ethical systems, including consequentalism, ethical relativism and the universalism of Plato, Aristotle, Kant and Aquinas. The consideration of these different moralities add depth to the understanding of central problems in ethical theory while demonstrating the methodology of doing philosophical ethics through logical analyses.

COURSE OBJECTIVES

(1) To weigh the ability of various ethics to be relevant; (2) To facilitate the student's ability to argue philosophically about appropriate values and norms both theoretically and in application to concrete situations; (3) To determine why one should be moral; (4) To offer an ethics that is helpful in achieving personal happiness and in promoting the common good; and (5) To enable students to determine why something ought to be done and why something ought not be done.
