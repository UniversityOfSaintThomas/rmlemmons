JOB OPENING FOR TENURE TRACK AND LIMITED TERM POSITIONS
AT THE UNIVERSITY OF ST. THOMAS, St. Paul, MN.


Two positions, one tenure track and one limited term (renewable annually for a maximum of five years), Assistant Professor or Instructor in the Department of Philosophy, beginning September 2000.  Salary and benefits competitive.  AOS (strongly preferred) – ancient philosophy (epistemology or metaphysics), or modern philosophy (epistemology or metaphysics), or contemporary analytic philosophy (epistemology or metaphysics), or continental philosophy, or non-Western philosophy.  AOC – open.  Candidates should be committed to sustaining and developing the Catholic intellectual tradition, taking account of the insights of contemporary thought.  In their letter of application candidates should discuss that commitment.  Teaching load: ordinarily five introductory courses in philosophy of the human person or ethics and one upper-division course per year.  Some non-teaching duties such as advising.  Ph.D. and teaching experience preferred; excellent teaching, writing, and philosophical skills required.  Applicants should indicate whether they wish to be considered for one or both positions.  The University of St. Thomas is a private, Catholic, comprehensive, liberal arts University with 5,000 undergraduate students.  The University is an equal opportunity employer and is committed to a program of affirmative action.  Women and persons of color are strongly encouraged to apply.  The University's Mission Statement and the Undergraduate College’s vision statement are available at http://www.stthomas.edu/www/Welcome.  Send Dossier (refer to #200673) including transcripts and a sample of written work to:  Philosophy Search, Department of Human Resources, University of St. Thomas, Mail #AQU217, 2115 Summit Avenue, St. Paul, MN 55105.  Review of mailed applications will begin on December 15, 1999.  Additional applications will be accepted at the Eastern Division meeting of the APA.
