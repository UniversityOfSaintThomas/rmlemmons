Questions concern Descartes Meditations 1, 2, 3 (on web), 5 and 6 found in Kessler; Descartes' Letters to the Princess (Kessler p. 470); and also Cole's "Mind, Body and Gender" p. 476 in Kessler

1a: Descartes argues that there is almost no sources of true and certain knowledge. Give one of his argument that some thing ----which seems certain---can be doubted.  2b: What is the first thing that cannot be doubted?

2: Descartes argues that if the self's existence cannot be doubted, then God must exist because nothing less than God could be the cause of the self's existence. Give one of his arguments that some thing cannot cause the self's existence. For example, why cann't parents cause the self's existence?

3: Descartes argues that if God exists, He must be good. Why couldn't God be evil?

4: In Descartes' Letters to the Princess (Kessler p. 470), Descartes answers various objections to his philosophy and explains how the body and soul interact. Give one of his explanations.

5: What is Cole's main argument?

6:  Feminists argue that traditional western philosophy has treated the human soul as an independent self. This is mistaken because the self is relational. If Cole were to encounter Aquinas would they agree on this point. Why or why not? (Remember, Aquinas not only argued that every living body is animated by only one soul, but that the self exists to love others as itself.)
