In the movie "First Knight," Lancelot must make several decisions about how to live his life and about what are the most important values to seek.  Lancelot makes four key decisions: (1) the decision to become a fighter who drifts from village to village;  (2) the decision(s) to rescue Genievere; (3) the decision to join King Arthur's Round Table; and (4) the decision to leave that Table.  These decisions are made according to Lancelot's values. Does Lancelot have one supreme value that never changes or does his values changes? What is most important to Lancelot? How does Lancelot view himself? What is his philosophy of life or his philosophy of person? Whom have we studied that would be most pleased with Lancelot's actions? Does Lancelot ever love King Arthur? Does King Arthur love Lancelot? Are there any critical differences between Lancelot and King Arthur? How does Lancelot differ from Malagant; Is this difference significant?Compare the values embodied in Lancelot's decisions to the philosophies of person presented in the handouts "The Drive to Give Oneself Away" and "The Metaphysics of Love." Did King Arthur, in "First Knight"  tap into this psychology of self giving when he created the Knights of the Round Table? Does Malagant, the evil knight and warlord, defy this philosophy of self giving?

In a short essay (one to two typed pages), consider the questions and events mentioned above. Write an argumentative essay that argues answering these two questions:
(1) At the beginning of the movie, would Lancelot would agree with the philosophies in "The Drive to Give Oneself Away" and "the Metaphysics of Love."  (2) At the end of the movie, has Lancelot changed his mind about the meaning of life and the drive to be self giving? (3) If movie had ended with Lancelot and Malagant arguing about their different philosophies of person, (a) what would have been the main bone of contention, (b) what arguments would they have used against each other, and (c) who would have made the better argument? Explain. Be sure to use specific instances to prove your points.
