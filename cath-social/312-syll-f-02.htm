Catholic social thought has always been focused upon caring for individuals. Beginning with Rerum Novarum in 1891, it became focused upon setting forth criteria for the political, cultural, social, and economical frameworks that protect and advance human dignity. It is thereby grounded not only upon a vision of the Church as having a role to play in the world but also upon the obligations of laity to transform the world according to the requirements of charity. This course seeks to explicate both of these groundings as well as to apply them to four contemporary problems: (1) freedom and the common good; (2) rights and the culture of death; (3) the development of pro-family policies; (4) work and economic justice.
