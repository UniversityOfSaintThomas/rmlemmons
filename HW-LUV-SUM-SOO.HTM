HONOR PLEDGE FORMS AVAILABLE TUESDAY. THE TAKE-HOME EXAM; ANSWER ANY TWO QUESTIONS. BE CONCISE BUT MAKE ALL NECESSARY CONNECTIONS (length should be approximately two typed pages total): (1) The dialogues of Plato contain arguments that the friendship of lovers is worse than the friendship of non lovers. Explain how Socrates/Plato argues this position and how Aquinas would argue against it.  (2) The chief problem for the Platonic understanding of love is the problem of desire. If love is desire, then love is necessarily bent upon self gratification; and, self gratification seems to involve selfishness and treating the beloved as a means. How are Plato, Aristotle, Aelred and Aquinas able to concede that love is desire while arguing that it need not involve selfishness and treating the beloved as a means?  (3) Equality and reciprocity seem to be intrinsic to the the best forms of interpersonal love. If this is the case, then friends receive what they give and vice versa. How then is spousal friendship---within a traditional relationship where the husband works and the wife cares for children---possible, when the difference in functions means that neither receives what is given and vice versa?  Do the philosophers thus far studied offer any  way for enabling spouses to become great friends? (4) Aristotle argued that friendship could not endure the distance between man and God; Aquinas argued that friendship with God is possible. Imagine that Aristotle and Aquinas were to meet in this life: how would Aristotle try to convince Aquinas that friendship with God was impossible; how would Aquinas try to convince Aristotle that friendship with God was possible?  (5) Racism and other forms of group prejudice express an attitude of "us" against "them."  Aquinas argues that likeness is a cause of love; and so it seems that racism as well as every form of group prejudice are loves caused by the similarity of sharing some characteristic, e.g. race.  Thus, it seems that Thomistic thought provides no basis for overcoming racism or any form of group prejudice.  Explain how would Aquinas argue against this pseudo-thomistic argument.
