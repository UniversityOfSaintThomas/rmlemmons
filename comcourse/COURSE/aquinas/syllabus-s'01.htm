PL 340: AQUINAS

Spring 2001



DESCRIPTION:

An introduction to the study of the Aquinas's philosophy as it bears on three questions: What does life mean? What should I do? What can I know? The first question requires determining whether God exists, whether happiness is possible, and whether human life has a destiny; these questions in turn asks whether there is an immortal human soul. The second question requires determining the nature of morality and its content. The third question requires determining whether truth is objective and whether there is any foundation to human knowledge. This latter question arises within each arena of exploration: determining the existence and nature of God and human beings and morality.



OBJECTIVES:

  • •To learn how to read and analyze the arguments of Aquinas
  • •To enhance skills of philosophical reasoning
  • •To increase appreciation for medieval culture
  • •To deepen understanding of the following issues: proofs for God's existence; the problem of pantheism; the problem of evil; the problem of the human soul's immateriality and immorality


GRADING

  • •60% two short papers (~3 pages) on two of the following topics: (1) knowledge; (2) God; (3) human nature; and (3) ethics. Each of these papers will use Aquinas's ideas and arguments to counter a contemporary argument.
  • •30% on seminar presentations
  • •10% on attendance and class participation
