Cultural Artifact

Pl 115.22; FALL  2000





The goals of this course include advancing one's logical skills and one's understanding of the American cultural view of the philosophy of person.

For your first cultural artifact present to the class some manmade object that represents either a dominant or a minority aspect of how American culture views the meaning of life and the identity of one's destiny as a human being. Your presentation should include a handout containing two arguments: (1) an argument that your artifact reflects an American cultural perspective; and (2) an argument that that your cultural artifact either promotes or hinders the truth about what it means to be a human being.   Your presentation should be insightful, reflective and well-argued. It should not last for more than five minutes.

 

Your second cultural artifact will present an object that represents either a dominant or minority aspect of how American culture views the nature of a human being, i.e. whether human beings consists only of their bodies or whether there is some nonphysical component to human nature.  Your presentation should include a handout containing two arguments: (1) an argument that your artifact reflects an American cultural perspective; and (2) an argument that that your cultural artifact either promotes or hinders the truth about human nature.  Your presentation should be insightful, reflective and well-argued.  It should not last for more than five minutes.
