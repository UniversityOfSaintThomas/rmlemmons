INTRODUCTION:

To wonder about the nature of love is to consider love in its origin, in its forms and in its activities. What causes love? How can there be different types of love? What makes love last? What destroys love? What kind of actions does love inspire? Can we be friends with God? Does friendship require equality? Does sex have an intrinsic interpersonal meaning? We pursue these questions primarily within the boundaries of philosophy as set forth by Plato and John Paul II.   In addition, significant historical developments in the philosophy of friendship will be considered.  This course will be structured as a seminar with students taking turns presenting analyses of the homework readings and posing discussion questions.

TEXTS:

  • Other Selves: Philosophers on Friendship edited by Michael Pakaluk
