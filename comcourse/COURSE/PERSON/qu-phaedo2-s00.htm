PHAEDO AND THE ARGUMENTS FOR IMMORTALITY
 
 
 

* Read pp. 35-67 (84e-118a); Focus Questions:
 

(1) Simmias argues that the body and soul are like the lyre and harmony. How does this argument by analogy attempt to show that the soul cannot be immortal?
 
 
 
 
 

(2) Cebes argues that the soul and the body are like the weaver and his coat. How does this argument attempt to show that the soul is not immortal?
 
 
 
 
 

(3) The effects of Simmias' and Cebes' arguments against immortality were so strong that Socrates' followers began to despair of ever proving the soul's immortality. What was Socrates' argument against such despair?
 
 
 
 
 

(4) Identify one idea that Socrates uses to refute Simmias' argument.
 
 
 
 
 

(5) In this autobiographical digression, what idea does Socrates use to argue against a materialistic view of the person?
 
 
 
 
 

(6) Identify one key idea that Socrates uses against the argument of Cebes.
 
 
 
 
 

(7) What surprised you while reading the Phaedo?
 
 
 
 
 

(8) Reflection question: If it weren't for Socrates, philosophy may never have recovered from the monism of Parmenides and the relativism of Protagoras. What was it about Socrates' convictions that made it possible for philosophy to become the way in which questions of soul, truth, beauty and goodness are investigated?
