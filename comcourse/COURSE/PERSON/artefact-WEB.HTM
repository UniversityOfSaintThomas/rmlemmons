Cultural Artefact

Pl 115

Spring 2000





This semester we have examined many issues concerning two questions: What are we? What ought we strive for? We have seen that the answer to the second question reflects the first: materialists argue that we ought to strive for the best comforts of this life, while dualists and hylomorphists argue that we ought to strive for the highest wisdom and goodness (even if it costs us worldly comforts). Aquinas argues that we love according to how we conceive of ourselves: if we consider ourselves to be our bodies, we primarily on materialistic values; if we consider ourselves to be our souls, we primarily focus on spiritual values without neglecting bodily goods. But how does our culture consider us? What values does it tell us to pursue? What assumptions does it make about human nature?



THE PRESENTATION: PLAN FOR ABOUT FIVE MINUTES

Select a cultural artefact that embodies any theme of this course. Present it to the class through the medium that expresses that artefact, i.e. play the song or the clip from a movie. On a handout, type the words of any lyric or dialogue. Then, on that handout, present a brief argument that your artefact does indeed reflect a theme from this course and either a dominant or minority aspect of American culture.



N.B. Be careful about identifying an artefact as presenting materialism because since neither hylomorphism nor Cartesian dualism requires abstention from life's pleasures, it is possible for an artefact to contain materialistic values without being evidence that materialism dominates American culture.
