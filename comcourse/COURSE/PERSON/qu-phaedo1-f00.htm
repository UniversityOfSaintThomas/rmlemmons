Read pp. 1-35 in the "Phaedo." Focus Questions:
 

(1) Why shouldn't one commit suicide?
 
 
 

(2) Why isn't Socrates grieved to die?
 
 
 

(3) Should a philosopher despise the body? Why is the body a prison?  What does it mean to say that philosophers are occupied by the practice of dying?
 
 
 

(4) Do the bodily senses assist in the acquisition of knowledge?
 
 

(5) Are absolute justice, absolute beauty and absolute good visible to the eyes? Can the essence of anything be sensed? Can the idea of equality be derived from unequal sensed objects?
 
 

 
(6) Does the soul's knowledge of essences or perfections require that the soul pre-exist the body? Why does the argument that answering questions successfully require the idea that all knowledge is the recollection of what was encountered before one was placed in the body?
 
 

(7) If all knowledge in this life is recollected from one's pre-existing state, then how come we do not know everything all the time?
 
 

 8) Dissolvability is a property of the compounded (things with parts) or the uncompounded (things without parts)?
 
 
 

(9) What are ghosts?
 
 
 

(10) List the reincarnations of various types of souls.
 
 
 

(11) What are the "nails" that rivets the soul to the body? Why are these "nails" successful rivets?
