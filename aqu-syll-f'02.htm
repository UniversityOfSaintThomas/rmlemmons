DESCRIPTION:

An introduction to the study of Aquinas’s philosophy as it bears on several questions: Who and what am I? What may I know? Is truth possible? What must I seek? What does morality require? How can love endure? What is the nature of love and friendship? Is there an omnipotent and providential God? Is Thomism important for faith? These questions divide the course into three segments: (1) the philosophy of the human person and epistemology; (2) ethics; and, (3) metaphysics and the philosophy of religion. Each of these segments investigates its questions in light of contemporary problems and issues.



OBJECTIVES:

•           To learn how to read and analyze the arguments of Aquinas.

•           To enhance skills of philosophical reasoning.

•           To deepen understanding of the Thomistic corpus.

•           To confront contemporary arguments challenging Thomism.



GRADING AND PAPER DEADLINES

•           10% Attendance, class participation, and home work.

•           20% Seminar presentations and argument analyses.                                                      

•           70% three short papers (~3-6 pages). Each of these papers will ascertain whether Aquinas’s ideas and arguments can withstand a contemporary argument to the contrary.

•                       Due Oct. 7th: First paper on topics in the philosophy of person and epistemology. Possibilities: Is hylomorphism a viable alternative to materialistic conceptions of person? Would clones be human or humanoid animals? Is brain “death” the death of a person? Does free will, determinism or compatibilism best describe human “choice”? Is truth possible? Is subjectivism viable? Are self-evident claims possible? What are the sources of human dignity?

•                       Due Nov. 4th: Second paper on topics in ethics. Possibilities: Does life have meaning? Is happiness possible apart from God? Are the natural last end and the supernatural last end reconcilable? Is America’s war on terrorism just? Is moral subjectivism viable? Are human rights viable? Does natural law underwrite Christian morality? Does natural law underwrite human law? What are the requirements of enduring love?

•                       Due Dec. 18th: Third Paper on topics in metaphysics and the philosophy of religion. Possibilities: Is pantheism a superior mode of explanation? Is atheism viable? Does the existence of evil suffice to disprove God’s existence? Does evolution eliminate the need for God’s causality? Must God be good, providential and omnipotent? Can faith be blind? Does charity override natural loves? Does Christianity need Thomism?
