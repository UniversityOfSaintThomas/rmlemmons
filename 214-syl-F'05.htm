Philosophical ethics not only explains and justifies morality, but seeks to establish the best basis for morality. The method of philosophical ethics is argument in search of insight; its hope is that evil will not triumph over the good. The overarching purpose of this course is to help us gain insight into ethical realities and consistency in moral reasoning. In addition, this course, while seeking to understand morality within and across cultures – emphasizes the insights of Aquinas and his natural law.

COURSE REQUIREMENTS AND GRADING

 


10% Class Participation. More than five absences subtracts one letter grade.


15% Class Preparation. At the beginning of each class, students are required to hand in a report on the text read for homework. These reports are not returned (so keep a copy for our class discussions and to help prepare for the quizzes). Late reports are not accepted. More than five missing reports subtracts an entire letter grade. These reports must be a product of your own work and not the work of another. These reports are not explicitly graded; however, excellent reports enable students to excel in class discussions, quizzes, and the in-class papers. Each report should attempt to answer the following questions: 1) What point(s), if any, were especially surprising, interesting, or disagreeable? Explain what made the point(s) an eye-opener. (2) What are the main points of the homework reading? 3) Should we agree or disagree with these main points? Explain. (4) Is the text a “keeper”? Explain. Your answers to these questions are especially important, not only because home work is the exercise that develops one’s mind, but because they are my way of knowing how difficult–and how interesting--you found the homework reading.



25% Wednesday quizzes — every week.


25% Midterm In-Class Papers. Topic to be assigned. An argument outline is required.


25% Final In-Class Paper: Issue Analysis and Policy Recommendation. This essay requires persuasively arguing that an act that we have not discussed in class is not only evil but ought to be opposed in some way. Objections to one's position must be explained and countered. An argument outline is required.


Extra Credit. Periodically, there will be talks given for which one can earn extra credit by reporting on the speech. The report should summarize the speech and then answer the questions required for the homework reports. Extra-Credit work, if well-done, warms my heart and makes it likely that I will curve borderline grades up. You may feel free to recommend that a talk count for extra credit.
