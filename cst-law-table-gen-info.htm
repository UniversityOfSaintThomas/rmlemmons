General Information on the Church

   Courtesy of Catholic University's Law Library

The Official Catholic Directory. New York: P. J. Kenedy, Annual.

Abstract: This annual publication provides information and statistics on Catholic dioceses, educational and charitable institutions, and priests and religious in the United States and its territories.

Catholic Internet Directories.

  http://www.shc.edu/theolibrary/dir.htm.

Abstract: This web site from the theology library at Spring Hill College is a gateway to a large number of Internet links on Catholic theology, culture, and practice.

 

Recommended Catholic Apologetic & Historical Books.

http://ic.net/~erasmus/RAZ57.HTM#II. CATHOLIC CATECHISMS.

Abstract: This Web page. authored by Dave Armstrong, provides a bibliography of books on Catholic Church history, institutions and doctrine.

 

Research Guides.

 http://www.lib.stthomas.edu/ireland/guides/index.htm.

Abstract: This web page contains PDF versions of a number of research guides to Catholic resources prepared by the reference staff of the Archbishop Ireland Memorial Library at the University of Saint Thomas in Minneapolis-St. Paul. Of particular note is the six page guide on Catholic Social Teaching.

 

Fox, Thomas C. Catholicism on the Web. New York: MIS Press, 1997.

Abstract: This resource is a detailed annotated bibliography of web sites dealing with the Catholic Church. Chapter 10 focuses specifically on issues of peace, justice and the environment.

 

McBrien, Richard P., editor. The HarperCollins Encyclopedia of Catholicism. New York: HarperCollins, 1995.

Abstract: This work is a single-volume encyclopedia of Catholicism. It contains many illustrations and a timeline of Church history. Many entries include bibliographies.

 

Welcome to the Catholic Church. Gervais, Or.: Harmony Media Inc., 1996.

Abstract: This computer optical disc includes an illustrated Catholic Bible, Catholic encyclopedic dictionary, all Vatican II documents, and other selections.

 

New Catholic Encyclopedia. New York: McGraw-Hill, 1967.

http://www.newadvent.org/cathen/.

Abstract: Prepared at the Catholic University of American, this standard reference work is, in essence, the second edition of the 1913 Catholic Encyclopedia.
